UNIVERSITY AND COLLEGE LIBRARIES OF OXFORD: New College

UO72. Indenture of books given by Nicholas Wykeham, 9 June 1407

  • UO72.1: Gregory the Great [c540–604, sedit 590–604]
    Moralia in Iob CPL 1708; ed. M. Adriaen, CCSL 143, 143A, 143B (1979–85).
  • UO72.3a: John Chrysostom [c347–407], patriarch of Constantinople
    [pseud.] Opus imperfectum in Matthaeum CPL 707; J. van Banning, CCSL 87B (1988); Stegmüller Bibl. 4350. [The tradition is largely English, and this work is much commoner in England than the authentic Homilies on Matthew.]
  • UO72.3b: William of Auvergne, known as Parisiensis [c1180–1249], bishop of Paris
    P. Viard in DS 6 (1967) 1182–92.
    De collatione et singularitate beneficiorum pr. in Guillelmi Alverni opera omnia (Paris/Orleans 1674), 2. 248–60; Ottman 7; Glorieux Rép. 141r.
  • UO72.4: John Chrysostom [c347–407], patriarch of Constantinople
    [pseud.] Opus imperfectum in Matthaeum CPL 707; J. van Banning, CCSL 87B (1988); Stegmüller Bibl. 4350. [The tradition is largely English, and this work is much commoner in England than the authentic Homilies on Matthew.]
  • UO72.5: Gregory the Great [c540–604, sedit 590–604]
    De cura pastorali CPL 1712.
  • UO72.6a: Quoniam fideles (25 August 1395), an encyclical letter from the chancellor and masters of the University of Paris concerning the Schism ???.
  • UO72.6b: Anselm of Canterbury OSB [c1033–1109], archbishop of Canterbury
    Orationes and Meditationes SAO 3. 3–75, 76–91, for authentic works. Manuscript collections varied widely and usually comprised both authentic and pseudonymous texts; for a 12th-cent. example, see T. H. Bestul, A Durham Book of Devotions (Toronto 1987). PL 158. 709–820 is a compilation based on later medieval collections; these tend to be far more extensive than those of the 12th cent. [See also Wilmart, Auteurs spirituels, 147–216.]
  • UO72.7: Bernard of Clairvaux OCist [1090–1153], abbot of Clairvaux
    De consideratione SBO 3. 393–493.
  • UO72.11: Gratian [† by c1160]
    Decretum PL 187; ed. E. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, vol. 1 (1879). [The ordinary gloss on the Decretum is that of Iohannes Teutonicus, revised in the mid 13th cent. by Bartholomew of Brescia: pr. Venice 1605; Kuttner, 103–115.] [`Paleae' are canons added to Gratian's original recension in the second, vulgate version.]
  • UO72.12: Guido de Baysio, known as Archidiaconus [†1313], archdeacon of Bologna
    Rosarium seu Apparatus ad Decretum pr. [Strassburg 1473] (GW 3744), &c.; Schulte, 2. 187–8.
  • UO72.13b: Tancred of Bologna (Tancredi da Corneto) [† c1236]
    Ordo iudicarius ed. F. Bergmann (Göttingen 1842); Kuttner, 431–2.
  • UO72.†13a: Goffredo di Trani [†1245]
    Summa super titulis Decretalium pr. [Cologne c. 1480] (GW 10949), &c.; Schulte, 2. 89–91.
  • UO72.†13c: Bartholomew of Brescia [†1258]
    Casus Decretorum, a reworking of Benencasa pr. Basel 1489 (GW 3426), Paris 1505, &c.; Schulte, 2. 84–5.
  • UO72.14: Gregory IX (Ugolino da Segni) [c1148–1241, sedit 1227–1241]
    Decretales ed. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, 2. 1–928; Schulte, 2. 3–25, 412. [The ordinary gloss on the Decretals is that by Bernard of Parma: pr. Strassburg 1468/71 (GW 11450), &c.; pr. Venice 1605; Schulte, 2. 115.] [Entries for Decretales ueteres are more likely to refer to one of the older decretal compilations; entered under Bernard of Pavia.]
  • UO72.14b: Iohannes Monachus (Cardinal Jean Le Moine) [†1313]
    Apparatus ad Sextum pr. Venice 1585; Schulte, 2. 192. [Often known as `Cardinalis'; his was treated as the ordinary gloss on the Sext at Paris but not at Bologna. See also under Liber Sextus.]
  • UO72.14b: Liber sextus Decretalium, continuing the Decretals from Gregory IX (1234) to Boniface VIII (1298) pr. Strassburg 1465 (GW 4848), &c.; ed. E. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, 2. 933–1124; Schulte, 2. 34–44. [The ordinary gloss on the Sext is that of Iohannes Andreae; at Paris that of Iohannes Monachus was preferred; the triple gloss also contained Guido de Baysio.]
  • UO72.15: Henricus de Segusio, known as Hostiensis [c1200–1271]
    Lectura super Decretales pr. Strassburg 1512 &c.; Schulte, 2. 125.
  • UO72.16: William de Mandagout [†1321]
    De electionibus pr. Paris 1506 &c.; pr. in Tractatus uniuersi iuris (Venice 1584–6), 15/1. 407v–435v; Schulte, 2. 183–5; DDC 5. 1077–8.
  • UO72.17: Innocent IV (Sinibaldo Fieschi) [sedit 1243–1254]
    Apparatus in quinque libros Decretalium pr. Strassburg 1478 (Goff I95), &c.; Schulte, 2. 91–4.
  • UO72.18: Willelmus Durandus the Elder [1237–1296], bishop of Mende
    Speculum iudiciale pr. Strassburg 1473 (GW 9148), &c.; Schulte, 2. 148–52; Lange, 2. 483–6.
  • UO72.19: Willelmus de Pagula [early 14th cent.]
    Summa summarum unpr.; excerpts ed. L. E. Boyle, Proceedings of Second Congress of Medieval Canon Law (Vatican City 1965), 415–56; Bloomfield 0234; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 800.
  • UO72.20: Henricus de Segusio, known as Hostiensis [c1200–1271]
    Summa super titulis Decretalium pr. Rome 1473 (GW 12231), &c.; Schulte, 2. 125–7. [F. Soetermeer, `Summa archiepiscopi alias Summa Copiosa: some remarks on the medieval editions of the Summa Hostiensis', Ius commune 26 (1999) 1–25.]
  • UO72.21: Liber sextus Decretalium, continuing the Decretals from Gregory IX (1234) to Boniface VIII (1298) pr. Strassburg 1465 (GW 4848), &c.; ed. E.   • UO72.22: Constitutiones Clementinae, collecting the constitutions of Clement V, promulgated by John XXII in 1317 ed. E. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, 2. 1129–1200; Schulte, 2. 45–50.   • UO72.23: Constitutiones Clementinae, collecting the constitutions of Clement V, promulgated by John XXII in 1317 ed. E. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, 2. 1129–1200; Schulte, 2. 45–50.   • UO72.26: Justinian [†565]
