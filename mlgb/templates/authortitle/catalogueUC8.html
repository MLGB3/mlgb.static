All data was derived from the List of Identifications (PDF) by Professor Richard Sharpe.

    Lectura in primam et secundam partem Digesti noui pr. Venice 1497–8 (IGI 5287); Savigny, 6. 279.

    Lectura in Codice pr. Strassburg c. 1475 (GW 7045), &c.

    G. Arrighi, Felino Sandei (1444–1503), Canonista e Umanista, Accademia Lucchese di scienze lettore e arti, Studi e testi 23 (Lucca 1987).
    Super Decretales pr. Venice 1497–9 (Goff S142); Schulte, 2. 351. But perhaps more likely the collection printed as commentary on the five books, ed. Jacques Mareshcal, [?Lyon 1513].
  • UC8.¶4: Franciscus de Zabarellis (Francesco Zabarella) [1360–1417]

    DBI 6. 640–69.

    Casus longi on Justinian's Codex, Digestum uetus, and Inforciatum pr. in three volumes, [Freiburg c. 1494] (Goff T558, T560, T562); pr. in three volumes, [Lyon 1490–97] (CIBN T408–410, Goff T559, T561).

  • UC8.¶11: Justinian [†565]
    Corpus iuris ciuilis, comprising Justinian's Digestum, Codex, Institutiones, and Nouellae pr. with the gloss of Accursius, Lyon 1584; ed. P. Krüger & others (Berlin 1868–95). [See also under individual titles.]
  • UC8.¶12: Gratian [† by c1160]
    Decretum PL 187; ed. E. Friedberg, Corpus iuris canonici, vol. 1 (1879). [The ordinary gloss on the Decretum is that of Iohannes Teutonicus, revised in the mid 13th cent. by Bartholomew of Brescia: pr. Venice 1605; Kuttner, 103–115.] [`Paleae' are canons added to Gratian's original recension in the second, vulgate version.]
