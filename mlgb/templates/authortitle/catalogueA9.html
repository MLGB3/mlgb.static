AUGUSTINIAN CANONS: Cirencester

A9. Leland, c. 1536-40

  • A9.*1: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    Gloss on the Psalms unpr.; Stegmüller Bibl. 1163; Hunt, Nequam, 134.
  • A9.*1x: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    Commentary on Proverbs unpr.; Stegmüller Bibl. 1164; Hunt, Nequam, 136–7.
  • A9.*1x: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    De muliere forti unpr.; Hunt, Nequam, 139; Stegmüller Bibl. 1166.
  • A9.2: Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    De Iesu puero duodenni ed. A. Hoste, CCCM 1 (1971) 249–78; Hoste, 51–4.
  • A9.3: Odo of Morimond OCist [†1161]
    DS 11. 624–7.
    Analytica ternarii, the final part of his Analytica, which circulated separately ed. H. Lange, CIMA 69 (1999); DS 11. 624–7.
  • A9.*4: Bede the Venerable [c673–735], monk of Wearmouth–Jarrow
    Commentary on Ezra and Nehemiah CPL 1349; ed. D. Hurst, CCSL 119A (1969) 237–392.
  • A9.5: Geoffrey Babio du Louroux [†1158], archbishop of Bordeaux
    Commentary on Matthew PL 162. 1228–1500 (as Anselm of Laon); Stegmüller Bibl. 2604, 7899 (as Stephen Langton).
  • A9.6: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    Laus diuinae sapientiae ed. T. Wright, RS 34 (1863), 355–503; Hunt, Nequam, 138.
  • A9.?*7: Robert of Cricklade OSA [† after 1188]
    Homiliae super Ezechielem unpr.; Schneyer Rep. 5. 171–6; Stegmüller Bibl. 7455.
  • A9.*8: Robert of Cricklade OSA [† after 1188]
    De connubio Iacob unpr.; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 532; Stegmüller Bibl. 7447.
  • A9.9: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    Commentary on the Song of Songs unpr.; Hunt, Nequam, 137. The work is sometimes referred to as Laus beatissimae uirginis (C. J. McDonough in Mediaeval Studies 66 (2004) 99–128), which was treated as a separate lost work by Hunt, Nequam, 130.
  • A9.*10: Alexander Nequam OSA [1157–1217], canon of Cirencester
    Speculum speculationum ed. R. M. Thomson, Auctores Britannici medii aevi 11 (1988); Hunt, Nequam, 137–8.
