UNIVERSITY AND COLLEGE LIBRARIES OF OXFORD: Corpus Christi College

UO27. List of Grocyn's books in alphabetical order of authors, in or soon after 1520

  • UO27.4: Scriptores historiae Augustae pr. with Suetonius, Milan 1475 (Bod-inc S-117), Venice 1489, &c.; ed. E. Hohl, Teubner (19712).
  • UO27.7a: Anselm of Canterbury OSB [c1033–1109], archbishop of Canterbury
    unspecified works
  • UO27.7b: Petrus de Crescentiis (Pietro de' Crescenzi) [c1233–c1320]
    Ruralia commoda pr. [Augsburg 1471] (GW 7820), &c.; ed. W. Richter & R. Richter-Bergmeier (Heidelberg 1995–8).
  • UO27.¶25: Concordantia maior, the so-called `third concordance' compiled by the Dominicans of Saint-Jacques, Paris pr. Strassburg, [not after 1474] (GW 7418) (under the name Conradus de Alemannia), &c.; Stegmüller Bibl. 1999, 3605–6; R. H. Rouse & M. A. Rouse in AFP 44 (1974) 5–30; Kaeppeli 755 (as Conradus de Halberstadt OP). Rouse & Rouse show that the work circulated in Paris by 1286; the attribution to Conrad is made only in the printed editions. [For the so-called `second concordance' or `English concordance', see Richard Stainsby.]
  • UO27.¶28: L. Iunius Columella [† after 65]
    De re rustica pr. in Scriptores rei rusticae, Venice 1472 (Goff S346), &c.; ed. V. Lundström & others (Uppsala 1897–1968).
  • UO27.¶36: Diomedes [4th–5th cent.]
    Ars grammatica pr. Venice [c. 1475] (GW 8399), &c.; GL 1. 299–529.
  • UO27.¶65: M. Fabius Quintilianus [† c100]
    Institutio oratoria pr. Rome 1470 (Goff Q24), &c.; ed. M. Winterbottom, OCT (1970).
  • UO27.¶69a: Pius II (Enea Silvio de' Piccolomini) [1405–1464, sedit 1458–1464]
    Epistulae familiares pr. Louvain 1477 (Goff P715), &c.; ed. R. Volkan, Der Briefwechsel des Eneas Silvius Piccolomini (Vienna 1909–12).
  • UO27.¶72b: Pelbárt Temesvári [c1435–1502]
    Sermones pomerii de tempore pr. Hagenau 1498 (Goff P254), &c.
  • UO27.¶83: Thomas Waleys OP [†1350]
    Expositio super duos nocturnos Psalterii (Pss. 1–38) pr. London 1481 (STC 19627), &c.; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 686; Kaeppeli 3890; Stegmüller Bibl. 8245. [Early edition someties confused with Diego Perez de Valencia.]
