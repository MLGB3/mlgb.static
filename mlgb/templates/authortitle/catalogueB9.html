  • B9.4: Itinerarium Antonini CPL 2330.
  • B9.5: Melito of Sardis [† c190]
    [pseud.] De transitu beatae Mariae uirginis CPG 1096; BHL 5351–2.

  • B9.7: Martin of Braga [†580], bishop of Braga
    Formula uitae honestae CPL 1080; Diaz 27; Bloomfield 4457.
  • B9.8: Ivo of Chartres [c1040–1116]
    De corpore et sanguine Domini, being the second part of his Decretum PL 161. 135–200.
  • B9.8: Ivo of Chartres [c1040–1116]
    Decretum PL 161. 47–1022.
  • B9.9: Historia Britonum, commonly attributed to Nennius ed. T. Mommsen, MGH Auct. Antiq. 13 (1898), 143–219; Lapidge/Sharpe 127–34. [The attribution to Gildas belongs to a distinct recension of the text. NB. The title Historia Britonum in medieval catalogues is more likely to refer to Geoffrey of Monmouth's Historia regum Britanniae.]
  • B9.10: Odo of Canterbury OSB [†1200], abbot of Battle
    Tractatus in libros Regum ed. C. de Clercq (Ventimiglia 1980); Stegmüller Bibl. 6058.

    Sermones largely unpr.; listed in Schneyer Rep. 5. 466–507 and by P. B. Roberts, Studies in the Sermons of Stephen Langton (Toronto 1968), 139–242.
  • B9.12: Richard of Melksham [?]
    Sermones unidentified.
  • B9.13: Stephen Langton [c1165–1228], archbishop of Canterbury
    Sermones largely unpr.; listed in Schneyer Rep. 5. 466–507 and by P. B. Roberts, Studies in the Sermons of Stephen Langton (Toronto 1968), 139–242.
  • B9.15: Alcuin [c735–804]
    Quaestiones in Genesim PL 100. 515–66; CMA Gallia, 2. 485–8; Stegmüller Bibl. 1085.
  • B9.16: Alcuin [c735–804]
    [dub.] `De proprietate sermonum' unidentified.
  • B9.†16: Alcuin [c735–804]
    Disputatio de rhetorica PL 101. 919–46; ed. C. Halm, Rhetores latini minores (Leipzig 1863), 525–50; CMA Gallia, 2. 159–62.
  • B9.†16: Isidore of Seville [†636], bishop of Seville
    De differentiis, comprising De differentiis uerborum and De differentiis rerum CPL 1187, 1202; Diaz 101. [The two works travel both separately and also together (as De differentiis Books I and II), but catalogue entries are not always distinct as to what is contained; CPL 1202 is the more common; ed. M. A. Andrés Sanz, CCSL 111A (2006); CPL 1187, ed. C. Cordoñer (Paris 1992). The title is not necessarily distinguishable in catalogues from the much rarer ps. Isidore, Liber differentiarum siue de proprietate sermonum, CPL 1226; anonymous collections of differentiae are likely to be unidentifiable.]
  • B9.17: Gerald of Wales [1146–1226]
    Itinerarium Kambriae ed. J. F. Dimock, RS 21/6 (1868), 3–152.
  • B9.17: Gerald of Wales [1146–1226]
    Topographia Hiberniae ed. J. F. Dimock, RS 21/5 (1867), 3–202.
  • B9.18: Gebeno of Eberbach OCist [13th cent.], monk of Eberbach
    Speculum futurorum saeculorum, a cento derived from Hildegard's Sciuias ed. J. C. Santos Paz, Millennio medievale 46 (2004).
  • B9.18: Hildegard of Bingen [1098–1179], abbess of Bingen
    Sciuias siue Visiones ac reuelationes ed. J. Lefèvre d'Éaples in Liber trium uirorum et trium spiritualium uirginum, Paris 1513 &c.; PL 197. 383–738; ed. A. Führkotter & A. Carlevaris, CCCM 43, 43A (1978). Manuscript copies in English libraries are more likely to be the much commoner cento derived from her book by Gebeno of Eberbach, Speculum futurorum saeculorum: ed. J. C. Santos Paz, Millennio medievale 46 (2004). On the manuscript tradition, M. Embach, Die Schriften Hildegards von Bingen. Studien zu ihrer Überlieferung im Mittelalter und in der frühen Neuzeit, Erudiri sapientia 4 (Berlin, 2003).
  • B9.19: Michael de Smaulfield [?]
    Summa unidentified.
