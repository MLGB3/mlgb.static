SECULAR INSTITUTIONS: Secular Colleges: Tattershall

SC314. Lincolnshire list, summer 1528

  • SC314.*1a: Roger of Waltham [† after 1332]
    Compendium morale unpr.; Bloomfield 1977, 5286, 5289.
  • SC314.*1x: Augustine [354–430], bishop of Hippo
    [pseud.] De assumptione beatae Mariae, inc. `Ad interrogata' (prol.), `Quia profundissime' (text) CPPM 2. 161; PL 40. 1141–8. The text is also found ascribed to Anselm and to Fulbert of Chartres.
  • SC314.*1x: Bonaventure OFM [1221–1274]
    Legenda Maior pr. in Legendae S. Francisci Assisiensis saeculis XIII et XIV conscriptae, Analecta Franciscana 10 (Quaracchi 1941), 555–652; Distelbrink 50; BHL 3107.
  • SC314.*1x: Gregory of Elvira [† after 392], bishop of Elvira
    (attrib.), De fide catholica CPL 554; ed. V. Bulhart, CCSL 69 (1967) 271–2; Lambert 315.
  • SC314.*1x: Hugh of Saint-Victor OSA [c1096–1141]
    De gratitudine beneficiorum Dei (Miscellanea I 99), abridged from his Misericordia et ueritas PL 177. 531; Goy, 452–7.
  • SC314.*1x: John Pecham OFM [c1240–1292]
    Philomena SBonO 8. 669–74; ed. G. M. Drèves, AH 50 (1907) 602–626; WIC 14071.
  • SC314.*1x: Marbod of Rennes [1035–1123]
    Agnitio duodecim lapidum preciosorum PL 171. 1771–2; WIC 2812.
  • SC314.*1x: Thomas Gascoigne [1403–1458]
    Tractatus de indulgentiis unpr.; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 658.
  • SC314.*1x: Visio S. Fursei
  • SC314.*2: John Lathbury OFM [†1362]
    Alphabetum morale siue Distinctiones theologicae unpr.; Stegmüller Bibl. 4758; Bloomfield 0117; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 273.
  • SC314.3: Robert Grosseteste, known as Lincolniensis [†1253], bishop of Lincoln
    De modo confitendi, inc. `Ad primum dicat sacerdos' ed. J. W. Goering & F. A. C. Mantello, RTAM 54 (1987) 52–112 (text, 80–110); Thomson, Grosseteste, 126; Bloomfield 0280.
  • SC314.4: Peter of Limoges [†1306]
    Oculus moralis pr. Venice 1496 &c.; Thomson, Grosseteste, 256–7; Bloomfield 5532. See also R. Newhauser in Exempel und Exempel-Sammlungen, ed. W. Haug & B. Wachinger (Tübingen 1991), 95–136.
  • SC314.*5: Richard Snettisham [†1416]
    Abbreviation of Robert Cowton's Commentary on the Lombard's Sentences unpr.; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 510–11; Stegmüller Sent. 735.
  • SC314.*5x: Peter Partridge [†1451]
    Tabula to Richard Snettisham's Abbreviation of Robert Cowton's Commentary on the Lombard's Sentences unpr.; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 432.
  • SC314.*6: Robert Grosseteste, known as Lincolniensis [†1253], bishop of Lincoln
    De impressionibus aeris seu De prognosticatione ed. Baur, 41–51; Thomson, Grosseteste, 103–104.
  • SC314.*6x: Augustine [354–430], bishop of Hippo
    [pseud.] De spiritu et anima pr, [Lauingen] 1472 (GW 2936), &c.; PL 40. 779–832; Bloomfield 4935. [There is no evidence to support the attribution of the work to Alcher of Clairvaux; on attibutions, D. Aschoff, `Der pseudo-augustinische Traktat De spiritu et anima', Revue des études augustinienne 18 (1972) 293–4. See also above, Augustine ps. De creatione primi hominis, for c. 35.]
  • SC314.*6x: Averroes (Ibn Rushd) [1126–1198]
    Compendia on Aristotle's Parua naturalia ed. E. L. Shields & H. Blomberg, CCAA 7, Medieval Academy of America 54 (1949).
  • SC314.*6x: De compositione chilindri, inc. `Inuestigantibus (naturae) chilindri compositionem' unpr.; Thomson, Grossesteste, 246; Thorndike/Kibre 776.
  • SC314.*6x: Hermann of Carinthia [early 12th cent.]
    Liber imbrium, inc. `Cum multa et uaria de nubium cognitione precelata Indorum tractaret auctoritas' ed. S. M. Low-Beer, PhD diss. (City University of New York 1979); Carmody, 85–7; Jenks 32; Thorndike/Kibre 319. The text is frequently transmitted with Japhar's Liber imbrium.
  • SC314.*6x: `Hermes'
    De quindecim stellis, Latin tr. ed. L. Delatte, Textes latins et vieux français relatifs aux Cyranides (Liège/Paris 1942), 241–75; Thorndike, `Astrological images', 224–7; Carmody, 56.
  • SC314.*6x: Iohannes de Sicca Villa [† c1295]
    De principiis naturae ed. R. M. Giguère (Montreal/Paris 1956); Sharpe, Latin Writers, 319.
  • SC314.*6x: John Waleys OFM [†1285]
    Breuiloquium de uirtutibus antiquorum principum et philosophorum pr. Lyon 1511, fols. 200v–216r, &c.; Glorieux Rép. 322b.
  • SC314.*6x: Practica geometriae, inc. `Geometrie due sunt partes principales' ed. N. L. Hahn, Transactions of the American Philosophical Society 72/8 (1982) 113–65; Thorndike/Kibre 585. [The opening passage of this text is almost identical to the Tractatus quadrantis ueteris; Thorndike/Kibre 0585.]
  • SC314.*6x: Raymond of Marseilles [fl. 1140]
    (attrib.), Liber iudiciorum ed. M.-Th. d'Alverny, C. S. F. Burnett, & E. Poulle, Raymond de Marseille. Opera omnia, vol. 2; Thorndike/Kibre 1184. The treatise is addressed to Robert de Beaumont (†1168), earl of Leicester.
  • SC314.*6x: Robert Grosseteste, known as Lincolniensis [†1253], bishop of Lincoln
    [pseud.] De quadratura circuli unpr.; Thomson, Grosseteste, 113, attributes to Grosseteste, but it is rather an excerpt from Simplicius on Aristotle's Physica, paraphrasing the version by Grosseteste, Quadratura per lunulas (Sharpe, Latin Writers, 551).
  • SC314.*6x: Robert Grosseteste, known as Lincolniensis [†1253], bishop of Lincoln
    [pseud.] Liber quadrantis unpr.; Thomson, Grosseteste, 261; Thorndike/Kibre 333.
  • SC314.*6x: Robert Kilwardby OP [†1279]
    Epistola ad Petrum de Confleto ed. F. Ehrle, Gesammelte Aufsätze zur englischen Scholastik (Rome 1978), 18–44; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 563.
  • SC314.*6x: Theorica planetarum pr. Ferrara 1472 (GW 10684), Venice 1478 (GW 10685), &c.; ed. F. J. Carmody, Theorica planetarum Gerardi (Berkeley, CA, 1942); Carmody, 167–8; Thorndike/Kibre 223. [O. Pedersen, `The Theorica planetarum literature of the middle ages', Classica & Medievalia 23 (1962) 225–32.]
