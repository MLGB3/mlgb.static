SECULAR INSTITUTIONS: Secular Colleges: Cobham

SC218. Bequest by Thomas Stone, 20 October 1464

3 identified entries found.

All data was derived from the List of Identifications (PDF) by Professor Richard Sharpe.

  • SC218.1: Vitas patrum. Collections with this common title invariably begin with Rufinus's translation (often ascribed to Jerome) of the Historia monachorum in Aegypto (PL 21. 387–462; ed. E. Schulz-Flügel [Berlin 1990]), and then go on to include other Lives and apophthegmata of the type collected and published by H. Rosweyde and reprinted in PL vols. 73–4. Jerome's Lives of St Paul the Hermit, St Hilarion, and St Malchus (CPL 617–19) also make a frequent appearance. [See also Historia monachorum in Aegypto; Jerome, Liber adhortationum sanctorum patrum.]
  • SC218.†2: Expositio uerborum difficilium legende, an anonymous work known from four extant copies, all English see note on SC218.
  • SC218.4b: Gesta Romanorum cum applicationibus moralizatis pr. [Cologne c. 1473] (GW 10082), &c.; ed. H. Oesterley (Berlin 1872 / repr. Hildesheim 1963); W. Röll in MLJ 21 (1986) 208–229; B. Weiske, Gesta Romanorum (Tübingen 1992).
