REGISTRUM ANGLIE

R39. Union catalogue by author and title, early 14th cent.

13 identified entries found.
  • R39.†1 (`de uirginitate beate Marie', 1 copy) = K5.9: Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    [pseud.] Rhythmus de laude uirginitatis pr. in Maxima bibliotheca ueterum patrum (Lyon 1677), 23. 165–9; Hoste, 108. [See also Maurice of Rievaulx, Rhythmus.]
  • R39.2 (1 copy) = K5.8: Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    [pseud.] Commentary on the Song of Songs not known to survive; Hoste, 104–5.
  • R39.3 (7 copies): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    [dub.] `De quattuor hominibus' unidentified.
  • R39.4 (`de xii oneribus in aduentu domini', 2 copies): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Sermones de oneribus PL 184. 817–24 and 195. 361–500; Stegmüller Bibl. 942; Hoste, 55–61.
  • R39.5 (4 copies): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Sermones de tempore et de sanctis. In England perhaps the most probable collection is that in Durham, UL, MS Cosin V. 1. 11 (s. xii), and three other copies ed. C. H. Talbot (Rome 1952); Hoste, 85–8. Also possible is the first Clairvaux collection: PL 195. 209–360; ed. G. Raciti, CCCM 2A (1989) 1–238; Hoste, 88–93. Other smaller collections are also found.
  • R39.6 (3 copies): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    De spiritali amicitia CCCM 1 (1971), 287–350; Hoste, 63–9. [See also Thomas of Frackenham.]
  • R39.7 (`de inclusis', 2 copies): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    De institutione inclusarum ed. C. H. Talbot, CCCM 1 (1971) 637–82; Hoste, 75–80. The last part of the text (§§29–32) circulated under the title Meditationes.
  • R39.8 (`super illud Cum factus esset Iesus annorum 12', 1 copy): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    De Iesu puero duodenni ed. A. Hoste, CCCM 1 (1971) 249–78; Hoste, 51–4.
  • R39.9 (1 copy): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Speculum caritatis ed. C. H. Talbot, CCCM 1 (1971) 5–161; Hoste, 41–49.
  • R39.10 (`dialogus', 1 copy): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Dialogus inter hominem et rationem not known to survive; Sharpe, Latin Writers, 29.
  • R39.11 (1 copy) = K5.7: Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    De anima ed. C. H. Talbot, CCCM 1 (1971) 685–754; Hoste, 81–2.
  • R39.12 (1 copy) = K5.19: Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Epistolae not known to survive. Walter Daniel's Life of Aelred, 41, refers to his many letters to important people.
  • R39.13 (1 copy): Aelred of Rievaulx OCist [1109–1167], abbot of Rievaulx
    Vita S. Edwardi regis et confessoris PL 195. 737–90; Hoste, 123–6; BHL 2423.
